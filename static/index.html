The Challenge

One of Earth's most biodiverse places is going largely unheard

The Pantanal stretches across 150,000 square kilometres of South America and is home to more than 650 bird species, countless amphibians, reptiles, and mammals. Yet much of it has never been systematically monitored. Seasonal flooding, wildfires, agricultural expansion, and remote terrain make traditional fieldwork slow and expensive.

Passive acoustic monitoring changes the equation. Place a recorder in the field and let it listen. The recordings capture the soundscape of an entire ecological community over hours or days. The challenge then becomes making sense of what was heard, at scale, without a biologist present for every file.

This tool does exactly that. Upload a 60-second recording and a deep learning pipeline identifies which species were vocalising, giving conservationists a faster path from data to understanding.

How it works

From raw audio to species identifications

A five-stage deep learning pipeline processes each recording. The whole process takes roughly 20 to 30 seconds.

1
Audio Input
60s recording split into twelve 5-second windows
2
Perch v2
Google's model extracts 1536-dimensional audio embeddings
3
ProtoSSM
State Space Model captures temporal patterns across windows
4
ResidualSSM
A correction layer refines the initial predictions
5
Results
Species ranked by detection confidence
